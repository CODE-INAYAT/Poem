Rain: A Paradox Profound

In whispers soft, the raindrops kiss the earth, A melody of life, each drop a birth. Yet thunder growls like beasts in midnight's gloom, Its roar an omen, shrouded in dark doom. The rain's embrace, a lover's gentle touch, Dancing on leaves, they sparkle just as much. But flash of lightning tears the sky in two, A jagged scar, a flash of blinding hue. Silken curtains drape the world in gray, A silver veil that hides the light of day. Yet winds will howl and whip the storm to might, Turning serene to chaos in the night. Each droplet sings a song of sweet refrain, A lullaby that calms the restless brain. But rivers swell with fierce, unyielding might, Flooding dreams, turning day into night. Oh rain, you are a paradox profound, In your beauty and your terror, we are bound. You cleanse the earth with every pure caress, Yet in your fury, hearts you do distress.
